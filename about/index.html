About

Musician, analyst, planner, and builder. I've been fortunate to move through very different worlds, and each one shaped how I think.

☉ IDENTITY

I'm Tony Scoburgh: a conservatory-trained musician, Army intelligence veteran, supply-chain planner, and builder of systems that put people first. I spent five years working in Arabic signals intelligence under a TS-SCI clearance, three years coordinating planning and master data work inside Procter & Gamble, and most of my life making music, teaching, and listening for how groups actually move together.

What connects all of it is the same instinct: I learn fast in opaque environments, I translate complexity into something people can act on, and I build the process layer that keeps teams from failing the people inside them. Right now I'm building public work under the name skele.t0l, studying organizational psychology, and looking for work and collaboration that use the full range instead of one narrow slice.

✦ WHAT I DO

I make work at the intersection of music, systems, and human coordination.

Most of what I do starts with the same question: how do you make something complex usable without draining the life out of it? That question shows up in different forms depending on the surface. Sometimes it looks like planning. Sometimes it looks like teaching. Sometimes it looks like music. Sometimes it looks like building a tool, a framework, or a world people can actually enter.

Professionally, I've worked in environments where clarity matters. In the Army, I served as a Cryptologic Language Analyst and SIGINT operator working in Arabic, turning raw signal into reporting that other people had to act on. At Procter & Gamble, I've worked in integrated planning and master data coordination, helping keep production aligned across materials, systems, and cross-functional teams. In both spaces, the through-line has been the same: read the board quickly, organize the signal, reduce failure points, and help people move.

Music is the oldest version of that instinct. I come from conservatory training in music education and oboe performance, and I still think like both a performer and an ensemble builder. I care about timing, listening, structure, and what it takes for a group to become more than a pile of individuals. That shows up in my creative work, in the way I teach, and in the systems I'm drawn to build.

The public name for that wider body of work is skele.t0l. Under it, I'm building music, tools, writing, and a larger creative framework called The Astral Compendium. The worldbuilding matters to me, but not as camouflage. It's there to give continuity and symbolic weight to the work, not to hide simple meaning behind lore. The point is still clarity: making something vivid enough to remember and solid enough to use.

I'm especially interested in work that sits between disciplines: strategy that needs taste, art that needs structure, systems that need empathy, and teams that need someone who can translate between worlds.
