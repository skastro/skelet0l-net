◈ About Me

I read the board quickly, organize the signal, reduce failure points, and help people move.

In the Army, I served as a Cryptologic Language Analyst and SIGINT operator working in Arabic, turning raw signal into reporting that other people had to act on. At Procter & Gamble, I work in integrated planning and master data coordination, helping keep production aligned across materials, systems, and cross-functional teams.

In both spaces, the through-line has been the same: take complexity, find structure, make it usable under real pressure. The music, the systems, the worldbuilding: different instruments, same practice.

The Arc
2014–2018

UC College-Conservatory of Music

Classical oboe performance. Four years of precision, ensemble discipline, and the deep patience that comes from double-reed work. Trained in analysis, arrangement, and the structures underneath music.

2018–2025

U.S. Army: Cryptologic Language Analyst

SIGINT operations in Arabic. TS-SCI clearance. Fort Hood/Cavazos. Turned raw intelligence into reporting under time pressure, within systems that fail people when coordination breaks down.

2024–Present

Procter & Gamble: Site Integrated Planner

Personal Health Care division, Cincinnati. Master data coordination, production alignment, and cross-functional planning across materials, systems, and teams.

The Bridge

Systems translator under pressure.

The creative work is real, but it's not the headline. What I do across every context is the same: enter a complex environment, learn its language fast, find the structural failure points, and build something that helps people move without losing what matters.

Music trained my ear for structure and detail. The Army trained my tolerance for ambiguity and my speed under pressure. P&G trained my fluency with systems, stakeholders, and interdependent timelines. They compound.
